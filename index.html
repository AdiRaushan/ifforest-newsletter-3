FROM THE CEO’S DESK

Welcome to the third edition of iFOREST Quarterly!

The second quarter of 2025 was marked by significant progress across our key focus areas—climate resilience, clean energy, and just transition. The highlight of the quarter was the launch of India’s first Integrated Heat and Cooling Action Plan (IHCAP) tailored for Bhubaneswar, developed by iFOREST in partnership with the Bhubaneswar Municipal Corporation and the Singapore-ETH Centre. This pioneering initiative positions Bhubaneswar as a model for climate-resilient urban heat management.

We also released three landmark reports. The Climate Cost of Air Conditioning presents the findings of a national survey across seven major cities, offering critical insights into residential AC use. Transforming India’s Coal Capital outlines a Just Transition Plan for Dhanbad and the Damodar Valley, addressing the socio-economic risks of declining coal and proposing a green industrial future. This is supported by our Toolkit on Repurposing Retired Thermal Power Plants.

In the clean energy space, keeping our sustained focus on expanding the potential of the Eastern Region, we released a comprehensive reassessment of Jharkhand’s renewable energy potential, identifying 77.5 GW of capacity across solar, wind, and biomass. A companion policy study offers pathways to address institutional and regulatory gaps, enabling Jharkhand to emerge as a renewable energy hub.

We are also proud to support the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy’s City Accelerator Program in Assam, in collaboration with Shakti Sustainable Energy Foundation and APDCL, to scale rooftop solar under the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana.

Through continuous research, partnerships, and public engagement—via media columns, podcasts, and explainer videos—we remain committed to advancing environmental action and policy.

Thank you for your continued support.

Sincerely,

HIGHLIGHTS

TRANSFORMING INDIA'S COAL CAPITAL

Jharkhand has an untapped renewable energy potential of 77.5 GW—far exceeding previous estimates. To support the state’s clean energy transition, iFOREST hosted a conference on August 23, 2025, in Ranchi and released three key reports: Transforming India’s Coal Capital, Jharkhand Renewable Energy Potential Re-Assessment, and Enabling Renewable Energy Growth in Jharkhand. These studies present a clear roadmap to shift coal districts like Dhanbad, Bokaro, and Ramgarh towards green economies by repurposing mining land, addressing policy gaps, and creating green jobs—positioning Jharkhand as a future renewable energy hub.

REPURPOSING THERMAL POWER PLANTS, TOOLKIT

What happens to coal power plants once they retire—do they simply sit abandoned, or can they be transformed into assets for a cleaner future? The Toolkit on Repurposing Retired Thermal Power Plants in India, developed by iFOREST provides a timely and practical roadmap to guide policymakers, utilities, and planners to decommission old coal- based power plants, prioritising the needs of workers and communities affected by plant closures—so that the shift to clean energy is both just and equitable.

NATIONAL SURVEY ON CLIMATE COST OF AIR CONDITIONING

What is the true climate cost of India’s exploding AC ownership? On World Ozone Day, 16th September 2025, iFOREST released findings from a national survey revealing a surge in AC use and overlooked refrigerant leakage driving climate and economic risks. The findings of the report The Climate Cost of Air Conditioning gather insights from over 3,100 households in Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Jaipur, Alahabad and Pune. It reveals that around 40% of ACs are refilled annually, costing consumers and the climate. In 2024, households spent ₹7,000 crore in refrigerant refills, releasing 52 Mt CO₂e into the atmosphere. The report urges urgent Lifecycle Refrigerant Management (LRM) and stronger servicing regulations to cut emissions and support long-term climate goals.
 

Iforest Launches India’s First Integrated Heat And Cooling Action Plan

National Conclave and Report Launch at Bhubaneshwar

Bhubaneswar is facing rising heat stress due to rapid urbanisation, declining green cover, and increasing cooling demand. iFOREST, in collaboration with the Singapore-ETH Centre, developed India’s first Integrated Heat and Cooling Action Plan (IHCAP) tailored for the city of Bhubaneswar. It was officially launched on September 2nd at a National Conference in the city. The plan is based on a year-long, data-driven study that assessed heat risks, identified vulnerable areas, and arrived at mitigation strategies using urban planning and nature-based solutions.

iForest explains - IHCAP Plan for Bhubaneshwar

In the coming decades, days of extreme heat will become the norm in Bhubaneswar, which saw over 230 days of heat alerts in 2024. iFOREST’s explainer film highlights this growing threat and explains the city’s comprehensive Integrated Heat and Cooling action plan, based on five pillars: Cool the City, Cool Buildings, Sustainable Cooling for All, Enhance Heat Resilience, and Adapt to Heat. Watch the video to understand more.

PARTNERSHIPS

iFOREST launches City Accelerator Programme in Assam


The Ministry of New and Renewable Energy has launched the ‘City Accelerator Program’ or CAP in Assam to implement the PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana, initially in Guwahati, Dibrugarh, and Barpeta. Assam ranks 9th nationally with 138 MW capacity installed across 42,525 households, but faces a 10% conversion rate vs. 25% nationally. CAP will bridge this gap by boosting demand, improving installations, and creating replicable models. With knowledge support from Shakti Sustainable Energy Foundation, iFOREST will provide technical support to APDCL and be the implementing partner in the roll out. The programme aims to boost rooftop solar adoption through capacity building, institutional support, and improving city-level policy and regulatory frameworks for APDCL, ULBs, and DISCOMs.

iFOREST partners with Assam Pollution Control Board


In the last quarter, iFOREST partnered with the Assam Pollution Control Board to strengthen environmental governance and support the development of clean air action plans for Guwahati and two other districts. The collaboration will target key pollution sources — open burning, dust, and industrial emissions from MSMEs — through city-level planning, capacity building for municipal bodies and workers, and policy support to refine regulatory guidelines. Together, we aim to deliver sustainable solutions for cleaner air across Assam.

A PEEK INTO THE NEXT QUARTER

Mapping Solar Potential in the East 

Rooftop and floating solar offer significant opportunities for states like West Bengal, Jharkhand, and Chhattisgarh to expand clean energy without large land requirements— In the next quarter, iFOREST will release comprehensive assessments on rooftop and floating solar potential across West Bengal, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, and Assam. These reports will offer state-specific data and policy frameworks to accelerate clean energy adoption and support a just, region-focused energy transition.

Media Workshop on Renewable Energy Potential

A media workshop is in the works in Bhubaneswar to build awareness on emerging solar technologies (Floating Solar PV, Agri-PV, and Rooftop Solar), wind and biomass. The session will equip journalists with new insights and build awareness on the opportunities and challenges of scaling solar, wind and biomass adoption eastern states. 

Eastern Region dialogue on FSPV 

iFOREST will host a regional event to convene policymakers, industry and experts on FSPV implementation and deployment in Eastern India. FSPV refers to solar panels installed in water surfaces. The discussions will highlight reservoir potential, financing needs, and pathways for scaling projects. 

The Financing Dialogue on Assam RTS 

To strengthen rooftop solar (RTS) adoption in Assam, iFOREST will organise a dialogue with financial institutions. The engagement will focus on RTS awareness, innovative financing models and risk mitigation measures to boost uptake. 

National Renewable Energy Flagship Conclave

Our annual National Renewable Energy Flagship Conclave will be conducted in Delhi, bringing together policymakers, industry leaders, financiers, and experts. The event will deliberate on India’s clean energy priorities and chart pathways for accelerated renewable adoption. 

Gender and Green Growth: Odisha’s Just Transition

In the upcoming quarter, iFOREST will release a report on barriers to women’s participation in Odisha’s green economy. The study maps the skilling landscape through a gender lens, reviews policy gaps, and puts forward actionable recommendations to expand women’s access to green jobs and leadership roles in the state’s just transition. 

Energy and Environmental Audit of Steam Boilers 

In the upcoming quarter, our team will conduct detailed energy and environmental audits of industrial boilers across the Delhi NCR region. The study will benchmark efficiency, emissions, and best practices to accelerate the transition toward cleaner industrial steam. 

Strengthening Climate and ESG Accountability in Steel 

This quarter, iFOREST will host a report release event to share the findings of two key studies and launch three new reports for India’s steel sector. The work focuses on developing a unified framework for GHG accounting and MRV, assessing ESG reporting among top listed firms, and strengthening BRSR disclosures with a steel-specific supplement—advancing transparency and accountability in the sector’s green transition. 

Ratnagiri District Development and Climate Action.

In the coming quarter, we will be preparing a District Development cum Climate Action Plan for Ratnagiri. This plan will bring together development priorities with climate action, creating a clear path for sustainable growth and stronger local preparedness for future challenges. Our focus is on a more robust, actionable, and locally owned framework for climate-resilient development.

Guwahati gets a clean air plan 

The report will focus on the city’s Hotspot Action Plan, prepared by combining existing literature with on-field survey data. The report will highlight targeted action items at key pollution hotspots to help reduce overall air pollution levels. 

A fix for stubble burning? 

The report for ICIMOD presents state-of-the-art stubble management methods, developed through literature review, on-ground surveys, and stakeholder consultations. It provides a curated list of key interventions to improve stubble management practices in India and Nepal. 

Ratnagiri District Development and Climate Action.

 The Odisha Just Transition and Climate Forum will serve as a multi-stakeholder platform to advance green skills, jobs, and entrepreneurship while supporting the state’s transition from fossil fuels. It will bring together CSOs, industry, PRIs, financial institutions, and policymakers to drive climate-resilient interventions, agriculture, and inclusive pathways for sustainable development.
